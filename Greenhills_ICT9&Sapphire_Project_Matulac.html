Fire safety awareness means analyzing the possible risks of fire in your vicinity and taking preventive measures to ensure minimum damage
and safe exit of all the person exposed to fire. Different fire safety awareness approaches are adopted at different locations. However, the
priority of every approach is the safe and successful evacuation of people trapped in a building in case of a fire. For efficient
fire safety awareness, proper fire safety training is crucial. A professional fire safety training program involves the necessary steps one must undertake
in the event of a fire. Well planned and practiced fire safety training can prepare you mentally and physically to keep your mind calm during an
emergency situation so that you can take the necessary steps to mitigate the damage.


Unplug items Unplugging certain appliances and electronics when they're not in use can help reduce the risk of electrical fires
and save energy. If an item is left plugged in for an extended period of time, it can overheat and potentially
cause a fire. This can happen with appliances like space heaters or toasters, which generate heat and
should be unplugged when not in use.
Use Surge Protections Using surge protectors can help reduce the chances of catching a fire by preventing power surges that can
happen due to lightning strikes, power outages, or problems with the electrical grid. Some surge
protectors come equipped with built-in circuit breakers that can trip and cut off power to the
connected devices in the event of a power surge, further reducing the risk of fires.
Never leave your
kitchen unattended
whilst cooking		 It's important to never leave your kitchen unattended while cooking because cooking is one of the leading
causes of home fires. By staying in the kitchen while you're cooking, you can monitor the food and the
cooking process, and take quick action if a potential fire hazard arises.
Store any flammable
products correctly		 We should store flammable products correctly because they can be a serious fire hazard if not handled
and stored properly. To store flammable products correctly, you should always follow the manufacturer's
instructions and any relevant safety guidelines.
